SMS Messaging Opt-In & Consent Documentation

Last updated: April 2026 | Aaron Coffey — SMS Automation Services for Local Businesses

This document describes how end-user consent is collected for each messaging use case operated by Aaron Coffey on behalf of local service business clients. All messaging is transactional and initiated by end-user action. No marketing or promotional messages are sent.

Use Case 1: Missed Call Follow-Up Text

How It Works

When a customer calls a participating local business and the call goes unanswered, the system sends a single follow-up text message to the caller. The message thanks the caller, apologizes for missing their call, and offers assistance.

How Consent Is Collected

The end user initiates contact by voluntarily placing a phone call to the business. By calling the business, the customer provides their phone number via caller ID and initiates a communication exchange. The follow-up text is a direct transactional response to the customer's initiated contact. This constitutes express consent for a transactional response to a customer-initiated inquiry.

Disclosures

The first text message sent to any new caller includes the following required disclosures:

Sample first message:

"Hi [Name], sorry we missed your call at [Business Name]! How can we help you today? Reply here or call us back anytime. Msg&data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help. Reply STOP to opt out."

Disclosures included in the first message:

  • Brand/business name identification
  • "Msg&data rates may apply"
  • "Reply HELP for help"
  • "Reply STOP to opt out"

Frequency

One initial follow-up message per missed call. If the customer replies, conversational responses continue only as long as the customer engages. Customers can opt out at any time by replying STOP.

Use Case 2: Website Contact Form Response

How It Works

When a customer submits a contact form on a participating business's website, the system sends a personalized email response and a follow-up text message to the phone number provided on the form.

How Consent Is Collected

The end user provides express consent by entering their phone number into the website contact form and checking the SMS consent checkbox. The checkbox is unchecked by default and is not required to submit the form. SMS consent is separate from any other terms of service or agreements. The form cannot be submitted with SMS consent unless the checkbox is actively checked by the user.

Sample Website Contact Form

Below is a representative example of the website contact form used by participating businesses. This form, or a substantially similar version, is implemented on each client's website:

By submitting this form, you consent to being contacted via email regarding your inquiry. Text message consent is optional and requires checking the box above. Your information will not be shared with third parties. Message and data rates may apply.

Disclosures at Point of Collection

The following disclosures are displayed at the time of phone number collection on the contact form:

  • Brand/business name identification
  • "Message and data rates may apply"
  • "Message frequency varies"
  • "Reply HELP for help"
  • "Reply STOP to opt out"
  • Link to Privacy Policy
  • Link to Terms of Service

Frequency

One initial response message. Up to 3 follow-up messages over 48 hours if the customer does not respond. Maximum 4 messages per form submission. Customers can opt out at any time by replying STOP.

Use Case 3: Post-Service Review Request

How It Works

After a customer completes a service with a participating business, the system sends a text message thanking them and requesting a Google review with a direct link.

How Consent Is Collected

The end user provides their phone number at the time of service (in-person, at check-in, or on a service intake form). At the time of phone number collection, the business provides verbal disclosure and a printed notice that the customer may receive a follow-up text message requesting feedback. The customer provides verbal confirmation of consent.

Verbal Disclosure Script

The following verbal script is provided to all participating businesses and is required to be used at the time of service when collecting the customer's phone number:

Required staff script:

"As part of our service, we may send you a text message after your visit today asking about your experience. Message and data rates may apply. You can opt out at any time by replying STOP to the text. Would you like to receive a follow-up text from us?"

Customer confirms verbally: "Yes" or "Sure" or equivalent affirmative response.

If customer declines: No text message is sent. Their phone number is not added to the messaging list.

Printed Notice

In addition to the verbal disclosure, participating businesses display the following printed notice at check-in, point of service, or on service intake paperwork:

[Business Name] — Customer Feedback Program

After your visit, you may receive a text message asking about your experience and inviting you to leave a review. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency: up to 2 messages per visit. Reply HELP for help. Reply STOP to opt out at any time.

Privacy Policy | Terms of Service

Frequency

One initial review request message per completed service. One follow-up reminder if no review is left within 48 hours. Maximum 2 messages per service visit. Customers can opt out at any time by replying STOP.

General Information

Opt-Out

Customers can opt out of all messaging at any time by replying STOP to any message. Upon opting out, they will receive a single confirmation: "You have been unsubscribed and will not receive any more messages from [Business Name]. Reply START to resubscribe. Reply HELP for help."

Help

Customers can request help at any time by replying HELP to any message. They will receive: "For assistance, please contact us at [Business Phone] or [Business Email]. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies. Reply STOP to opt out."

Privacy

All messaging opt-in data and consent information is kept confidential and will not be shared with any third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. See our full Messaging Privacy Policy for details.
